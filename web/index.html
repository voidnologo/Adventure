The Setting

A World Torn Between Forces

Jazz clubs and tommy guns. Airships and arcane eruptions. A world where the familiar collides with the impossible.

The Age of Eruption

Within living memory, the Veil — the boundary between the mundane world and something vast and inhuman beyond it — thinned, and magic flooded in. Ancient power warped landscapes, spawned creatures of legend, and granted terrible gifts to those who dared to reach for them.

The world is not Earth, but it is familiar. The cultures, technology, and social structures mirror the 1920s closely enough that you can ground yourself without a 50-page gazetteer. Then magic breaks through the floor and nothing is familiar anymore.

The tone is heroic. Players are good people doing good things in a world with real evil. Cultists resurrect dead gods. Beasts of legend stalk the wild zones. But the darkness is something to stand against, not wallow in.

The Veil

Casters draw Aetheric power through the Veil. Where it's thin, magic is strong and wild and poorly understood. Every spell cast leaves residue that makes machines less reliable. In the deep wild zones, technology simply ceases to function.

The Engine

Pushing back is the Galvanic force — drawn from the Engine, the metaphysical wellspring of human industry and will. Arc guns, voltaic pistols, and aetheric resonators channel this counter-resonance to dampen spellcasting. Whether the Engine is a conscious force, nature's immune response, or something humans manufactured — nobody knows. People debate it. Cults form around the question.

The Bedrock

A steel blade works everywhere. A horse doesn't care about aether. Smart adventurers never go anywhere without a sword — because it's the only thing that's never betrayed anyone.

Design Philosophy

What This Game Is

⚖

Theater of the Mind

No minis, no maps, no grids. A rulebook, character sheets, and a handful of dice around a table. Everything plays in the imagination.

⚙

Rules-Lite but Meaningful

A one-page cheat sheet covers 90% of play. Depth emerges from decisions, not complexity. No bloat, no subsystems for every situation.

⚔

OSR Lethality

Combat is dangerous. The death spiral means getting hurt forces real tactical decisions. Think before you fight.

❖

Magic is Wondrous & Wild

Magic is new, poorly understood, and dangerous. It inspires awe and fear in equal measure. Power always has a price.

★

Mechanical Consistency

One core resolution system. d100 roll-under, always. No special-case subsystems — everything works the same way.

✍

Rulings Over Rules

Concrete mechanical anchors for the GM to interpret flexibly. Solid mechanical floors, then trust the table to adjudicate.

How It Works

Core Mechanics

One resolution system. One dice type. Everything else flows from there.

The Core Roll

d100 roll-under. Roll equal to or under your target number to succeed.

Skill Target = ((10 + stat) × level) + 30 Example: Melee at level 3, PC +2 ((10 + 2) × 3) + 30 = 66 Roll 66 or under to hit.

01 is always a critical success. 00 is always a critical failure. Higher skill? Wider crit range.

The Death Spiral

Damage fills wound tiers. Each tier stacks a penalty on everything you do.

TierStatusPenalty
1OKNone
2Harmed-10%
3Maimed-25%
4Incapacitated-50%

Wounds and exhaustion penalties stack. Being Harmed and Weakened means -20% to everything.

Two Paths of Magic

Cast a spell. Roll for power. The margin determines the tier.

MarginTier
1-10Weak
11-30Standard
31-50Strong
51+Spectacular

Scholarly casters choose to accept or dial down. Wild casters ride the wave and take what the magic gives them.

The Interference

The Veil erodes technology. The Engine erodes magic. The balance slides back and forth.

Each net point of Aetheric accumulation: -2% technology Reliability Guns jam. Engines stall. Radios die. Each net point of Galvanic accumulation: -2 to casting target numbers Conventional weapons? Immune. A steel blade works everywhere.

The world exists on a spectrum from industrial dead zones (no magic) to deep wild zones (no tech).
