## Key Design Decisions

1. **Setting:** 1920s-flavored secondary world (not Earth). Magic vs technology tension is the core setting mechanic — they interfere with each other, justifying both melee weapons and firearms.
2. **Tone:** Heroic, not nihilistic. PCs are good people fighting real evil. "We can make the world better." Real darkness exists (cultists, dead gods, corrupted magitech) but players have moral purpose.
3. **Classless system:** Classes are descriptive archetype labels (like Shadowrun), not mechanical restrictions. Point-buy skill system.
4. **Magic philosophy:** Primal and wild, recently arrived, poorly understood. Two paths — scholarly (control, reduced backlash) vs. wild (unpredictable power spikes). No dark magic for players; that's villain territory.
5. **OSR lethality with death spiral:** Wound penalties (-10%/-25%/-50%) make getting hurt meaningful. Death saves when incapacitated. Character death is expected but not constant.
6. **Theater of the mind only:** No minis, maps, movement ranges, or cones. Fully narrative.
7. **Progression:** Skill-use driven (Mouse Guard / Symbaroum style), not XP-for-kills. Details deferred.
8. **Scope:** City noir investigations + wilderness expeditions. NOT a dungeon crawler.
